About

What is this?

Grind-Zone is a medication timing and mood tracking tool built around real pharmacokinetic data. Enter when you took your pill — see exactly what zone you're in, when your peak hits, and when things wind down. Log how you feel throughout the day and the app slowly learns your personal timing.

Why it exists

Built for personal use. Couldn't find anything that actually explained what was happening in your brain at 2pm vs 8am, or what Wellbutrin and Remeron are doing to each other when both are active. So this got built instead.

Medications covered

  • Ritalin LA — stimulant, bimodal release (IR + delayed beads), two distinct peaks, ~10hr window
  • Concerta 27mg — stimulant, OROS osmotic pump, continuously ascending curve, peak at 6–9hrs
  • Wellbutrin XL — NDRI antidepressant, long single arc, peak zone 5–14hrs, 21hr half-life
  • Lamictal 50mg — mood stabilizer, sodium channel blocker, 29hr half-life, cumulative therapeutic effect
  • Remeron 7.5mg — NaSSA antidepressant/sleep aid, peak sedation at ~2hrs, half-life adjusted for biological sex (26hr male / 37hr female)

Combination tracking

When Wellbutrin and Remeron are both active, the app detects the interaction state and shows a live ⚗️ x + y = z panel — what the two drugs are doing to each other right now, which neurotransmitters are elevated, what to expect, and what to watch for. Based on published pharmacodynamics research including Blier 2010 (46% vs 25% remission RCT) and mirtazapine α2 blockade mechanisms. Logs made during combo state are attributed to the combo, not either drug individually.

Feeling log + adaptive calibration

Log how you feel (1–10) at any point in the day. After 6 entries with elapsed time data, the app starts calibrating your personal peak timing — using exponential decay weighting (recent entries count more), a pharmacokinetic anchor prior (defaults resist change until real data outweighs them), and per-drug shift ceilings based on published Tmax variability ranges (±2hr for Ritalin/Concerta/Wellbutrin, ±1.5hr for Lamictal/Remeron). Zone boundaries, the now-banner, and the timeline all update to reflect your data.

Log organization

Logs are grouped by day (3am rollover — late nights stay with the right day) or by cycle phase. A 30-day calendar strip shows feel score dots per day. Filter by feel level, tags, or notes. The day view shows your dose schedule as badges. The cycle view groups by zone — useful for seeing patterns like "I always score low during Trough."

Accounts + sync

Sign in with a magic link (no password). Logs sync to a private Postgres database via Supabase — Row Level Security enforced at the database level, not just app code. Dose times stay local (localStorage) since they're session-specific. Guest mode available — logs stay in browser only.

Technical notes

  • Single HTML file — no framework, no build step, no dependencies
  • Backend: Supabase (Postgres + Auth)
  • Adaptive algorithm: weighted histogram, exponential decay, pharmacokinetic prior, per-drug Tmax ceiling
  • Day boundary: 3am rollover — doses clear automatically, new day starts fresh
  • 30-second live ticker for zone updates
  • Shareable URL — encodes your dose schedule as query params

Disclaimer

Not medical advice. Pharmacokinetic timings are based on published data and vary between individuals. Adaptive calibration reflects your logged experience, not clinical guidance. Always follow your prescriber's instructions.
